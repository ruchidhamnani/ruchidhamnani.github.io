Undergraduate Research (B.Tech Project)
Prof. Sandip Chakraborty
PARIMA: Viewport Adaptive 360-Degree Video Streaming

As a part of the project, we developed an end-to-end viewport-adaptive 360-degree video streaming system using state-of-the-art viewport prediction model. This model can predict the future viewport of the user (that is where the user would look next) and send that part of the 360-degree frame at higher quality and rest of it at lower quality. This would improve Quality of Experience (QoE) and reduce bandwidth consumption.
Here are some procedures involved:

  • We exploited video contents in terms of object trajectories derived using a robust spherical object tracking algorithm. Our claim was that the viewport of the user depends upon the trajectory of the prime objects in the video along with the past viewport.
  • We designed a unique augmentation of Passive-Aggressive Regression and ARIMA time-series models for viewport prediction. We implemented the model to be online in nature, adaptive to user preferences dynamically and effective for streaming.
  • We compared the model performance with state-of-the-art baselines and observed an improvement of QoE by over 30%. We proved that our video streaming system is superior than the baselines through a variety of experimentation results.

Domain: Systems, Video Streaming, Image Processing, Machine Learning, Time Series Analysis
